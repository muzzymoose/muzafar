Customer Segmentation Analysis

Role Data Scientist
Duration 2 Months
Tools Python, K-Means, RFM

Overview

Built customer segments for a UK online retailer using RFM analysis and K-Means clustering. The model identified 5 distinct behavioral groups, enabling targeted marketing strategies and more efficient resource allocation.

The Challenge

The retailer treated all customers uniformly with blanket campaigns, leading to wasted marketing spend and missed personalization opportunities. They needed a data-driven way to identify high-value customers versus those at risk of churning.

Approach

  • Calculated RFM scores (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) for each customer
  • Applied robust scaling and outlier handling to normalize features
  • Used elbow method and silhouette analysis to determine optimal cluster count
  • Validated segments with domain expertise and business logic

Key Findings

  • Top 20% of customers (Champions + Loyal) generate 65% of revenue
  • 22% of customers are "At Risk" — previously valuable but declining
  • Targeted campaigns achieved 35% higher click-through rates
  • Marketing spend waste reduced by 28% through better audience targeting
