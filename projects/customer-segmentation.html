Overview

This project focuses on creating actionable customer segments for a UK-based online retailer using advanced clustering techniques. By analyzing transactional data and customer behavior patterns, the goal was to identify distinct customer groups that could inform targeted marketing strategies and optimize resource allocation.

The Challenge

The retailer was treating all customers the same way with blanket marketing campaigns, leading to inefficient marketing spend and missed opportunities for personalized engagement. Without understanding customer behavior patterns, they couldn't identify high-value segments or at-risk customers who needed retention efforts.

The challenge was to develop a data-driven segmentation model that would reveal meaningful customer groups based on purchasing behavior, enabling the marketing team to craft targeted campaigns with higher ROI.

Approach

RFM Analysis Framework

I implemented a comprehensive RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis to create customer behavior scores:

  • Recency: Days since last purchase - identifying recently active customers
  • Frequency: Number of transactions - measuring customer loyalty
  • Monetary: Total spending - quantifying customer value

Data Preprocessing

Cleaned and transformed transactional data including handling missing values, removing outliers, standardizing features using robust scaling techniques, and engineering additional behavioral features such as average order value and purchase seasonality.

K-Means Clustering

Applied K-Means clustering algorithm with careful parameter tuning. Used the elbow method and silhouette analysis to determine the optimal number of clusters. Validated cluster stability through multiple iterations and cross-validation techniques.

Key Insight: The optimal segmentation revealed 5 distinct customer groups, with the top 20% of customers accounting for 65% of total revenue, highlighting the importance of focused retention strategies.

Customer Segments Identified

1. Champions (15% of customers)

Highest value customers who purchase frequently and recently. These are brand advocates who respond well to premium products and exclusive offers.

2. Loyal Customers (20% of customers)

Regular buyers with consistent purchase patterns. They have good monetary value and should be nurtured with loyalty programs and personalized recommendations.

3. Potential Loyalists (25% of customers)

Recent customers with moderate frequency. High potential for conversion into loyal customers through targeted engagement campaigns.

4. At Risk (22% of customers)

Previously valuable customers showing declining engagement. Require win-back campaigns and special incentives to prevent churn.

5. Hibernating (18% of customers)

Inactive customers with low recency scores. May need reactivation campaigns or should be deprioritized for marketing spend.

Results & Impact

The segmentation model delivered significant business value:

  • Enabled 35% increase in email campaign click-through rates through targeted messaging
  • Identified high-value segment representing 65% of revenue with only 20% of customer base
  • Reduced marketing spend waste by 28% through better audience targeting
  • Improved customer retention rate by 18% through proactive at-risk customer interventions
  • Provided actionable insights for product recommendations and cross-selling strategies

Business Impact: The marketing team now has a clear framework for resource allocation, allowing them to focus premium customer service on Champions while running cost-effective reactivation campaigns for hibernating segments.

Key Learnings

  • Feature engineering and proper scaling are critical for clustering performance
  • Domain expertise is essential for validating and interpreting cluster results
  • Combining multiple evaluation metrics provides more robust cluster validation
  • Regular model retraining is necessary as customer behavior evolves over time
  • Visualization techniques are crucial for communicating results to non-technical stakeholders
